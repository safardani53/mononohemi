    TIME certainly flies, and classic celebrities from the 1990s like Bridget Fonda, Jonathan Taylor Thomas, Lucy Lawless, and more are a stark reminder of just that.

    These notable faces from way back when will have you doing a double-take and take you back to yesteryear.

    Jonathan Taylor Thomas

    Quite the heartthrob in the '90s, Jonathan is best known for his role in the long-running sitcom Home Improvement, as well as classic films from the decade including Man of the House, Wild America, and Tom and Huck.

    The actor was also the voice of young Simba in the iconic 1994 animated film The Lion King.

    Jonathan, who turned 40 years old in September, was recently photographed in public for the first time in eight years.

    Bridget Fonda

    Bridget, 58, was an action star in the '90s as the killer assassin in Point of No Return.

    The daughter of Peter Fonda also showed off her leading lady chops in Single White Female and It Could Happen To You.

    The retired actress was spotted in Los Angeles recently for the first time since 2009.

    The reclusive star wore a black-and-white shirt, black-rimmed glasses and baggy pants, as she stepped out of her silver Land Rover SUV.

    She shares her 17-year-old son, Oliver, with her husband/composer Danny Elfman, 68.

    Lucy Lawless

    For six seasons Lucy kicked butt on the TV series Xena: Warrior Princess.

    However, while the show wrapped up in 2001, Lucy has still appeared in numerous roles, including quite the memorable performance in 2004's EuroTrip.

    Lucy, who recently stunned fans with a rare selfie, has gone on to appear in other popular programs including Salem, Ash vs Evil Dead, and Parks and Recreation as Ron Swanson's love.

    Brendan Fraser

    Quite the box office draw in the '90s, Brendan certainly had plenty of people swooning in various movies, most notable in George of the Jungle.

    The star also appeared in Encino Man, Blast From the Past, and in the box office hit The Mummy - as well as its two sequels.

    Earlier this month, the 52-year-old made a rare public appearance on the red carpet for the premiere of No Sudden Move, as he shocked fans with his head-turning transformation.

    Lara Flynn Boyle

    The actress cemented herself in the minds of '90s fans forever with her role as Donna Hayward in the cult series Twin Peaks, however, she also made her mark on other projects.

    Lara, 51, is also known for her role in the popular series The Practice, as well as films Wayne's World, Baby's Day Out, and the first two Men in Black films.

    The actress, who once dated iconic actor Jack Nicholson, was spotted walking her dog recently in a rare appearance.

    Amanda Bynes

    If you were a kid in the '90s and owned any television at all, chances are you came across this star, as she owned the decade.

    Amanda was a huge star as a teen thanks to her roles in hit kids shows All That and The Amanda Show, which led her to take her talents to the big screen in the early 2000s.

    From Big Fat Liar and What a Girl Wants to She's the Man, Amanda continued to reel in the hits before bowing out of Hollywood in the late 2000s to work on her mental health and to battle her struggles with drugs.

    Macaulay Culkin

    He was accidentally left home alone in 1990, which led him to become one of the biggest stars of the '90s before he was even allowed to see a PG-13 film.

    Starring in Home Alone and its sequel, as well as other films like My Girl, The Good Son, Richie Rich, and The Pagemaster, Macaulay dominated the decade before hopping out of the public's eye for some time

    The 40-year-old, who recently welcomed his first child with Brenda Song, will appear in Ryan Murphy's American Horror Stories later this summer.
