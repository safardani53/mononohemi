    WHEN it’s hot outside there’s nothing nicer for your dog than a cool dip in a crystal clear pool – and the best dog paddling pools make this an easy and affordable summer treat.

    When the weather is hot, dogs are prone to overheating and this can escalate to heatstroke (which in rare cases, can be fatal), so a paddling pool is a great way to help keep your dog safe in the heat. Splashing around in a paddling pool is also just great fun when it's just too hot to chase a ball.

    When shopping for the best dog paddling pools, there are a few things to consider, including size and material.

    For example, if you've got a puppy and not much outdoor space at home, you may only need the smallest paddling pool, but, if you buy a bigger model for your dog, the kids can even play in there too (though of course, it’s not likely to stay crystal clear for long!)

    Look for pools that don't need inflating – children's inflatable pools will be easily punctured by your dog's teeth and claws, so you need to buy a pool made with materials designed to withstand canine play. Materials such as PVC with fibreboard panels inside for the body of the pool and toughened, non-slip PVC for the base are the best options for puncture-proof dog paddling pools.

    Read on for our round-up of the best dog paddling pools to buy in the UK this summer.

    What are dog paddling pools?

    It's exactly what you think it is. A small pool to install in your garden, made for your dog to bathe, swim and play in when the weather's warm.

    Instead of the inflatable paddling pools you often find for kids, dog paddling pools are even easier to set up.

    Typically made from scratch-proof PVC, the designs easily fold open and shut, can be transported with ease and don't require an air pump to assemble.

    But that's not all. These paddling pools make excellent fun for children too, and can even double up as sandpits, ball pits and even baby baths. They also make ideal dog baths and a good place to brush your pooch down after a long walk.

    Dog paddling pools aren't the only way to help pups from overheating in the warm weather: you'll also find cooling mats for dogs online, which are typically gel-filled and pressure-activated to start cooling your pup once they step on it.

    Do dogs like paddling pools?

    Most dogs love to splash about in the water, so a paddling pool just for them will likely go down a treat.

    Even if your dog isn't that much of a water baby, during the summer and warmer weather, a cool place to chill out will be welcomed by hot pooches.

    Are paddling pools good for dogs?

    Paddling pools are a good way for dogs to have fun and keep cool, but options that are not meant specifically for dogs may not be the best idea.

    This is because dogs may scratch or burst some pools (particularly those aimed at young children). So, when buying a pool for your pup, it's always best to purchase one that is designed with dogs in mind..

    How much do dog paddling pools cost?

    The dog paddling pools in our roundup cost from £15 upwards, but of course price varies. Small pools cost less than large versions and you will need to be guided by the size of your dog when choosing which one to buy.

    Don’t be tempted to buy an inflatable child’s paddling pool though as your dog will likely put a hole in it with his teeth or claws while playing and replacing it will cost more in the long run.

    Look on it as an investment that should last longer than one summer. The best dog paddling pools are another way to help keep your pooch cool during hot weather – along with walking them during the coolest times of the day, supplying plenty of water to keep them hydrated and providing a cooling mat to rest on.

    Where to buy dog paddling pools in the UK

    Puncture-proof paddling pools for dogs have grown in popularity in recent years so you shouldn’t have problems finding them online or in town, even supermarkets stock them during the summer months.

    What to look for in a dog paddling pool

    When choosing a dog paddling pool, make sure you're buying one that is the right size for your dog, as well as the space you have available.

    It's also best to buy pools that are specifically meant for pets, rather than people (although people can use pet paddling pools, too!). This is because pet pools are generally more durable.

    If your pooch is particularly rambunctious, it may also be worth spending out a little bit more on a more heavy-duty option to avoid any breaks or bursts.

    There are many different colour options and sizes available and some dog paddling pools even easily fold away for storage purposes. This may be handy for pet owners who struggle for space or travel with their pup a lot.
