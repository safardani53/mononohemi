    Chris Hadfield Biography

    Chris Hadfield was born on a corn farm in Sarnia, Ontario, to parents Roger and Eleanor, and at the age of nine, he witnessed the Apollo 11 moon landing on television. He was a sky instructor before becoming an Air Force test pilot. He attended White Oaks Secondary School in Oakville, Ontario, and graduated from Milton District High School in his senior year. He joined the Air Force immediately after graduating from high school.

    Career

    At the age of 15, he received a glider pilot scholarship from the Royal Canadian Air Cadets. He joined the Canadian armed forces immediately after high school and attended Military College. He earned a degree in mechanical engineering there.

    He received formal training and flew tactical fighter planes, as well as an exchange program at the US Air Force Test Pilot School. At the University of Tennessee Space Institute, he earned a master’s degree in Aviation Systems. During his time there, he flew nearly 75 different aircraft.

    Hadfield was one of four Canadian astronauts chosen. He was involved in the development of many space stations. For 25 missions, he was NASA’s chief CAPCOM, the voice of mission control to space astronauts. From 2001 to 2003, he was NASA’s Operation Director at the Yuri Gagarin Cosmonauts Training Centre (GCTC) in Star City, Russia. He was NASA’s Chief of Robotics and the International Space Station Operations, Director. One month after his last mission in 2013, he announced his retirement at the age of 35.
