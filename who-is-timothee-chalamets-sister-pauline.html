    HBO'S hit series, The Sex Lives of College Girls features the sister of actor, Timothée Chalamet.

    Here's everything we know about the life and career of Pauline Chalamet.

    Who is Pauline Chalamet?

    Pauline Chalamet is an actress, dancer, and director born on January 25, 1992, in New York City.

    At 10 years old, Chalamet danced in Broadway's production of A Midsummer Night's Dream.

    Pauline attended Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School where she majored in drama.

    Following high school, Chalamet graduated from Bard College where she double majored in political science and theater.

    Acting hits close to home as Pauline made her debut into the film industry playing the role of Joanne in Judd Apatow's The King of Staten Island.

    In 2020, Pauline co-founded Gummy Films, a production company.

    The following year, the actress was cast on Mindy Kaling's HBO show, The Sex Lives of College Girls.

    Who are Pauline and Timothée's parents?

    Pauline and Timothée come from a long line of creatives.

    Their mother, Nicole Flender is a real estate broker at The Corcoran Group.

    However, before Nicole got into real estate, she was a Broadway dancer having previously taught dance.

    Chalamet's father is an editor for UNICEF. The French-born editor paved the way for both Timothée and Pauline's bilingualism.

    As for their love for the film industry, their uncle Rodman Flender is an actor, writer, director, and producer.

    Throughout his career, Flender has a long line of film credits including The Office, Gilmore Girls, and The O.C.

    Where does Pauline live?

    Upon graduating, Pauline Chalamet packed her bags and moved to Paris without telling her family until after she signed her lease.

    Though Pauline primarily lives in Paris, she often finds herself back and forth from what feels like home to her actual hometown.

    Chalamet told NYLON, "The advice I was getting was, basically, ‘Paris is great and you can live there, but if you're interested in things going faster, you have to come to the U.S.
