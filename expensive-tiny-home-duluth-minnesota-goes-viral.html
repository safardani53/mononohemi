    A TINY home has gone viral after people learned about its wildly expensive price tag and the home's layout.

    The home, located in Duluth, Minnesota, had more than 10,000 views within its first day of being posted on a realtor website.

    For a whopping $195,000, the one-bedroom, one-bathroom home is only 205 square feet totaling a cost of $951 per square foot.

    Compared to other homes on the market in the Duluth area, a 1,339-square-foot home is currently on the market for $199,000 - that's just $192 per square foot.

    According to Rocket Mortgage, a tiny home is normally about $300 per square foot, making it $30,000 to $60,000 per home.

    The home is described as a "great choice" for investors and "sustainably-minded" buyers" on the listing.

    "The tiny home is thoughtfully designed to maximize space and minimize environmental impact. With off-street parking, you'll enjoy the convenience of having your own dedicated space."

    Photos from Laura Jean Media Services give an inside look at the home, showing a kitchen complete with a dishwasher, stove, full-sized fridge, and a washer/dryer.

    There is also some counter space available that can be doubled as a work area if needed.

    Next to the kitchen is the living room which can fit a wall-mounted TV and a small couch.

    Located above the kitchen in a lofted space is the bedroom that can fit what appears to be a queen-sized bed in the photo and an end table.

    However, the area of the home which has drawn the most attention is the bathroom, where the sink basin is connected to the toilet to save space.

    The home is described as "an excellent opportunity for passive income generation" and that someone can "embrace sustainable living and leave a smaller carbon footprint with this eco-friendly home."

    A TikToker known as @Zillowtastrophes featured the home on their account leaving commenters shocked.

    "You lost me at 'your sink is also your toilet'," wrote one commenter.

    "Just buy a shed from Home Depot and live in it," wrote another person. "Cheaper."

    A third person wrote: "Tiny homes should come with a tiny price."
