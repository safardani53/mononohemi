    'This is not football' - Bitter Rodri launches savage attack on Scotland after his Spain side lose 2

    MANCHESTER CITY star Rodri launched a scathing attack on Scotland after they defeated Spain on an epic night at Hampden. Man Uniteds Scott McTominay was the hero for Steve Clakes side for the second-straight Euro qualifier.

    26 Aug 2024, 00:00

    MANCHESTER CITY star Rodri launched a scathing attack on Scotland after they defeated Spain on an epic night at Hampden.

    Man United’s Scott McTominay was the hero for Steve Clake’s side for the second-straight Euro qualifier.

    After his matchwinning two-goal display over Cyprus, the midfielder grabbed another brace as Scotland beat the Spaniards 2-0 in front of a raucous Hampden crowd on Tuesday.

    Rodri, 26, was captained La Roja and struggled to contain his frustration during the post-match interview as he accused the Scots of gamesmanship.

    While speaking to Viaplay, he claimed that the home side’s approach was “a bit rubbish” as he also blasted the officials.

    Rodri fumed: “For me, it's a bit rubbish because it's always wasting time. They provoke you. They always fall.

    “This is for me not football. The referee has to take part of this and he says nothing.

    “It's frustrating because we want to win, because you waste time, you waste time. But they have their weapons and we will learn for the next time.”

    Scotland’s Andy Robertson was booked in the opening period following a clash with Pedro Porro that resulted in the Spain defender going down clutching his face.

    The visitors then also had a number of penalty appeals waved away - with veteran Spain forward throwing himself to the ground in a tantrum.
