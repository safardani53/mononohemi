    Career

    He rose to prominence after winning second place in the Operalia competition in 1999. The following year, he made his Italian debut as des Grieux in Manon at the Teatro Carlo Felice in Genoa. In 2000, he made his debut as Macduff at the Berlin State Opera. In 2003, he performed as Rodolfo at the Glyndebourne Festival Opera in England. He performed as Alfredo in Verdi’s La Traviata at the Salzburg Festival. Carlo Rizzi conducted the piece, which was directed by Willy Decker.

    In 2006 and 2008, he shared the stage alongside Netrebko and Domingo in Berlin’s Waldbuhne and Vienna’s Schonbrunn Palace. He made his Berlin State Opera debut in Bohuslav Martinu’s Juliette or the Circle of Songes. In 2017, Rolando sang his first Ulisse in Claudio Monteverdi’s opera, II ritorno d’Uliesse, in pairs. In 2021, he returned to New York’s Metropolitan Opera in the role of Papageno. Most recently, in 2022, he made his international debut as Loge in Richard Wagner’s Das Rheingold at Berlin State Opera in Dmitri Tcherniakov’s new staging. Christian Thielemann conducted it. He was named creative director of the Mozartwoche festival in 2018, and his contract will expire in 2028.
