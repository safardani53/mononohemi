    GMA's TJ Holmes has hired NYC's ‘Lawyer of the Year’ Robert H. Moses to battle his ex-wife Marilee Fiebig in divorce court after his "affair" with Amy Robach, The U.S. Sun has learned.

    The 45-year-old GMA anchor filed for divorce on Wednesday, December 28 in New York after 12 years of marriage.

    TJ hired Robert H. Moses of Moses Ziegelman Richards & Notaro, LLP. In December 2022, he was named NYC's "Lawyer of the Year" in Best Lawyers: The Family Law Issue 2023.

    He received 4.8 out of five stars on Martindale-Hubbell ratings. He's also been featured on Super Lawyers, an exclusive list of top-rated attorneys, since 2006.

    Per his attorney profile: "Rob is known for his pragmatic approach to the law, helping countless clients resolve their family disputes economically, thoughtfully, and fairly.

    "His clients range from celebrities and high net worth individuals in financial, publishing, sports, and other fields to those of more modest means."

    Rob joined the boutique matrimonial law firm as a partner in 2011.

    Their office is located in the Empire State Building in Manhattan.

    'TOP-RATED'

    Rob is also a Fellow of the American Academy of Matrimonial Lawyers and is part of the Board of Managers.

    He received his law degree from St John’s University Law School in June 1989 and has been a lawyer since 1990.

    Most read in Entertainment

    Prior to that, he received his Bachelor of Science degree in Business Administration from New York University where he graduated cum laude.

    TJ's ex Marilee, for her part, does not have an attorney listed at the moment; she has yet to speak out about the 'affair' and has only posted on social media.

    The former couple shares one child together, a nine-year-old daughter called Sabine.

    TJ also has two children, Brianna and Jaiden, from his previous marriage.

    NOT HIDING THEIR AFFECTION

    TJ and Amy were last spotted at Atlanta's Hartsfield-Jackson Airport on Monday, December 26.

    DailyMail shared exclusive photos of them looking "relaxed" as they walked through the airport.  

    TJ and Amy, 49, have reportedly been dating since earlier this year. News of them having an 'affair' broke last month.

    They since have been taken off the air while an investigation is being conducted into their relationship.

    The U.S. Sun exclusively revealed that the new couple was going to be "pushed out" of the morning show. 

    A source close to the production of the family-oriented ABC news program said execs and top talent were furious over co-stars Amy and TJ's alleged romance, which has rocked the GMA staff and thrown production into absolute chaos. 

    COMING BACK?

    The source said: "There is no way they are going to be able to stay at GMA. 

    "They may not be outright fired, but they'll be relegated to lesser roles, which will make it undesirable for them to continue."  

    The U.S. Sun also was the first to report that Amy has moved out of the $5.2 million marital home she once shared with her husband Andrew Shue.

    Amy and Andrew have yet to file for divorce. 

    Us Weekly was the first to report the news of TJ's divorce filing and Radar Online was the first to report the news of Robert H. Moses representing him.
